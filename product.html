Industry context

The pharmaceutical industry has always been a relatively defensive industry and agnostic to economic cycles. Aging demographics, the advent of genomics and the application of big data are driving further innovation in the pharmaceutical industry and the need for capital. This is driving larger pharmaceutical groups and medium-sized biotech companies to seek product divestures in order to refocus their efforts on their pipeline of asset development and their organisations to focus on their larger blockbuster drugs. There-in lies an opportunity to execute a buy and build strategy in the pharmaceutical space where the focus in not necessarily on large geographic coverage and growth but in carefully selecting assets from an array of opportunities available that offer (1) high barriers to market entry either through complex manufacturing needs of the drug, (2) small and niche patient populations and (3) drug adminstration and prescribtion protocols that deliver a high degree of brand loyalty. In this respect, building a diverse product portfolio that not only is diversified across therapeutic areas and offers growth but also has an associated higher tail value when many of these assets come off patent; making it difficult for generic competitors to enter and as a result offers a higher tail value post the respective patented lifetime.

Why we are investing

Attractive Entry Valuation

CAT is being purchased for an overall consideration of US$31.8mn of upfront consideration and a deferred payment of US$33mn to be funded with the business’s own cash flows. The upfront consideration being paid represent 50% of the value of the inventory the business holds in finished goods and API. Furthermore, at 1.3x 12-month forward EV/EBITDA this represents a significant discount the 5-6x that similar businesses trade on public markets and previously private listed comparables have transcated; offering a high margin of safety to our original investment.

Experienced, Well-Aligned Sponsor Partner

Our sponsor partners in the transaction are Stanley Capital (Link to their profile) and are joined by a senior industry executive as an operating partner to the assets who was formlery a C-level executive at a private equity backed USD250mn EBITDA generic buybuild pharmaceuticals business. As a signal of their committment to the transaction, Stanley Capital and its senior advisors are investing 10% of the total equity required as part of the initial transaction with a higher than industry standard co-investment % going forward. We believe this level of investment from our partners provides a differentiated alignment within the opportunity.

Strong underwriting of a great platform investment

As part of the structured transaction with the vendors, we have managed to secure US$39mn worth of free inventory which will be provided to the business for free of cost. This represents a ~50% discount to our on-going cost of goods sold, increasing the margin of the business and our EBITDA to US$24mn in 2020E from US$11.1mn in 2019. CAT itself was originally acquired and coupled with an exceptional senior management team as a platform to a speciality pharma buy and build strategy of a large pharmaceutical listed business. Due to activist investor pressure we have been presented this platform as our anchor corporate transaction which we will utilise to transact predominantly pharmaceutical product acquisitions as bolt-ons. We have already identified our intial bolt-on in Project KITTEN.

About CAT

Company Description

• Cat is a global specialty pharmaceutical company focused on two niche hypertension products, namely Tekturna and Tekturna HCT – both are unique options for patients who are intolerant to the mainstream products on the market.

• Both products are out of patent and declining, but are complex to manufacture giving it some defensive characteristics vs generics

• 64% of Cat’s 2020E net revenue are generated outside of the US spanning 13 countries and the company has authorized CMO partners in multiple continents US revenue is split into branded products and authorized generics (“AG“)

• Cat is currently owned by a listed pharmaceutical holding and has historically focused on higher margin countries and has a typically outsourced organisation with 15 full-time employees

• Cat was bought in a 2016 carve-out from Novartis and is based in Dublin.

Investment Profile

We target a minimum hold period of 5 years to realize the build and build strategy’s full potential. Our base case underwriting, assuming a close by September of 2020 delivers a net IRR after fees of 29.4% and a multiple on invested capital (MOIC) of 3.13x. We have a high degree of confidence in being able to secure an alternative source of API for CAT’s core portfolio product and in doing so we believe that the higher IRR of 32.69% and 3.49x MOIC can be quite achievable.

What should I Consider?

Technology transfer risk

In many cases of product acquisitions in the pharmaceutical industry there are certain tech transfer risk associated with such transactions between one manufacturer and the other.

Inventory and great operating partners

We have given ourselves enough run-way with stock and inventory on balance sheet. Two senior pharma executives who are non-executive board members have been appointed to these matters.

M&A risk

Given the strategy of making further bolt-on acquisitions, there are always inherent risks with additional mergers and acquisitions of products

New hire and history of well structured transactions

The chief business officer of a US$250mn EBITDA business has been recruited to complement the team and our sponsor partners history of structuring transactions with large margins of safety gives us comfort.

Clinical trial risk

In order to realise the upside to the business case, we need to invest capital in research and development in order to secure additional market opportunities for our assets.

Deep commercial and clinical assessment before any spend

The team would be looking to take a deep probability adjusted view on the opportunity to expand the label on any of the products before any R&D capital is spent.
