Investment methodology

A proven thematic approach to origination by targeting secular growth sectors and companies with repeatable transformation stories across technology, healthcare and sustainability.

Value Creation

Stanley Capital’s deep thematic approach to investing and industry knowledge in the sub-sectors they invest in brings about transactions that can be structured on bilateral basis and thereby allowing for very attractive relative entry valuation. Value creation then after is driven through earnings growth focus achieved by revenue growth, performance optimisation and market consolidation.

Culture

Cultural focus on bringing a deep network of proven industry executives to advise and co-investment into deals. Partnering with our management teams and these executives we focus on building a more sustainable and successful culture.

Expected Returns

Deal-by-deal alignment to drive exceptional returns in excess of 20% IRR target. Stanley Capital partners invest a high portion of their net worth in each of the opportunities they pursue and there is a great deal of alignmnet between investors and Stanley Capital.

Simon Cottle

Founding Partner
Former Head of UK Buyout and Partner at Ardian

Simon has led 7 private equity investments with an attributable track record of 2.7x / 38% IRR (4 deals realised) & unrealised track record of 3.6x / 44% IRR (3 deals), deploying approximately $950m USD of equity between 2013 and 2019.

Simon originated and led his first independent pharma buyout deal Essential Pharma in December 2019 and has made further 5 investments totalling a further $800m USD of equity investment since 2008 as a junior.

Simon’s lead investor experience was as a Managing Director at Ardian and Director at Hg Capital. He was named as one of the top 40 under 40 private equity practitioners in the Financial News awards in 2013. At Ardian, Simon built the UK team from 2 to 6 full time professionals.

Simon’s pre investing background was as a Natural Resources investment banker at Deutsche Bank and at Citigroup in Equity Capital Markets. Simon was a British Olympian in the Athens Olympic Games in 2004 (Rowing) and holds an MBA (INSEAD) and an MA (Oxford) where he was a Richard Blackwell Scholar and multiple College Prize winner in 1997 and 1998.

James Brooks

Founding Partner
Founding Partner Former Head of UK & Ireland for GS MBD

James is a proven private equity investor and c-suite executive leader with a strong track record in transformative value creation in energy and technology-related sectors.

From 2016 – 2020, James co-led the rapid growth of Lightsource BP to become a global leader in the renewable energy sector with investors benefiting from over 20x equity returns. In that same period, James has founded, raised and worked as Managing Partner of one of the world’s largest emerging market-focused green infrastructure funds, the $700m Eversource Capital, and completed investments across utility scale and distributed renewable energy, mobility and waste management markets in India.

Prior to joining Lightsource BP, James had an extensive career in private equity across energy, industrial, telecoms and technology sectors. Most recently he was co-head of EMEA Energy and UK for Goldman Sachs’s Merchant Banking Division, its principal investment platform.

James received his Masters and BA degree in Physics from Merton College, University of Oxford.

Patrick Harguett

Partner
Former IP at Centerbridge Partners

Patrick is a career private equity and special situations investor with a strong track record in both equity and debt transactions (including loan-to-own deals).

After starting his career in investment banking at JP Morgan, Patrick was a private equity investor at Centerbridge Partners and Monarch Alternative Capital with significant Board responsibilities and experience in successful platform buy and builds.

In 2019 Patrick originated, fundraised and has been leading since his first independent buyout deal with a total enterprise value of €370m, which is due to close in 2020.

Patrick received his Masters in Mathematics at the Technical University Munich with summa cum laude. He published the specialist book “A Fractional Heath-Jarrow-Morton Approach for Interest Rate Markets“ while on a post-graduate research scholarship at Imperial College London.

He is a competitive tennis player and a passionate angel investor including an early-stage investment in one of Europe‘s leading challenger banks.
